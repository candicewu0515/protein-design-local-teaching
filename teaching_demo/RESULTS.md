# Generate, then Verify — a reproducible, GPU-free teaching protocol for the protein-design loop

> **Article type:** education / technical note (a *computational protocol*, **not** a
> research-findings paper). It demonstrates and makes fully reproducible the
> *generate → verify* workflow on a laptop; it does **not** claim a new protein, a new
> method, or experimental validation.
>
> **One-line scope:** every "✅ self-consistent" verdict below means *one model
> (ProteinMPNN/RFdiffusion) proposed a sequence/backbone and an independently trained
> predictor (ESMFold / AlphaFold2) agrees it should adopt the target fold* — i.e.
> **in-silico self-consistency, which is not proof that the protein folds in a tube.**

**Tools** (all on one Apple-Silicon Mac, CPU/MPS, no CUDA):
ProteinMPNN (`v_48_020`, seed 37, T=0.1) and RFdiffusion (`2d0c003`) for *generation* ·
ESMFold (`facebook/esmfold_v1`, single-sequence) and ColabFold/AlphaFold2 (`1.6.1`,
MSA-based) for *prediction/verification*.
**Proteins:** 1L2Y Trp-cage (20 aa) · 1VII villin HP36 (36 aa) · 1PGA protein G B1 (56 aa)
· 1UBQ ubiquitin (76 aa).

Full environment, versions, commands, and metric definitions are in
[`METHODS_AND_REPRODUCIBILITY.md`](METHODS_AND_REPRODUCIBILITY.md); every number here is
regenerated by [`analyze_structures.py`](analyze_structures.py) → [`data_summary.csv`](data_summary.csv).

---

## 1. What this protocol does (and the variant used)

The canonical design loop is *RFdiffusion → ProteinMPNN → AlphaFold/ESMFold*. RFdiffusion
needs CUDA and is unreliable on a Mac, so the **main** teaching track uses the
**"redesign a known backbone"** variant, which is pedagogically better because the native
experimental structure provides a measurable target:

> **native backbone → ProteinMPNN (writes a NEW sequence for that fold) → predictor
> (does the new sequence fold back to the target shape?)**

The pure *de novo* loop (RFdiffusion invents a backbone from noise) is included as a
secondary track (§5), and we also ran it locally on CPU.

Two distinct model *types* appear, which is the central teaching contrast:
- **Generation** (ProteinMPNN, RFdiffusion): many sequences → one fold.
- **Prediction** (ESMFold, AlphaFold2): one sequence → its structure.

## 2. Step 0 — predictor sanity check on the NATIVE sequences

Before trusting a predictor as a *verifier*, we check it reproduces the four known
structures from their real sequences. We run **both** predictors so students see the
single-sequence vs MSA-based difference:

| PDB | protein | ESMFold pLDDT | ESMFold CA-RMSD | AF2 (ColabFold) pLDDT | AF2 pTM | AF2 CA-RMSD |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1L2Y | Trp-cage (20 aa) | 91.1 | 0.51 Å | 94.9 | 0.13ᵃ | 0.69 Å |
| 1VII | villin HP36 (36 aa) | 92.1 | **2.12 Å** | 87.6 | 0.50 | **2.18 Å** |
| 1PGA | protein G B1 (56 aa) | 88.3 | 0.49 Å | 96.8 | 0.81 | 0.29 Å |
| 1UBQ | ubiquitin (76 aa) | 90.5 | 0.83 Å | 95.9 | 0.84 | 0.80 Å |

<sub>ᵃ pTM (like TM-score) is undefined-in-spirit for very short chains; AF2's own pTM for the
20-aa Trp-cage is 0.13 despite pLDDT 94.9 — read pLDDT for tiny proteins, not pTM.</sub>

**Reading (deliberately bounded):** on these four small (≤76 aa) single-domain proteins,
both predictors recover the experimental fold at high confidence. Three observations a
student can take straight from the two columns:
- **1VII is the hard case for *both* predictors** (ESMFold 2.12 Å, AF2 2.18 Å), and AF2
  *correctly* lowers its own confidence there (pLDDT 87.6, its lowest of the four). So the
  borderline result is a property of this frayed-terminus helical bundle, not a quirk of
  one model — independent confirmation, not a defect to hide.
- **MSA helps where homologs exist:** on protein G B1, MSA-based AF2 reaches 0.29 Å vs
  single-sequence ESMFold's 0.49 Å — a concrete "single-sequence vs MSA" teaching contrast.
- With *n = 4* over a narrow size range, this establishes only that the predictors are
  *usable verifiers at this scale*; it is **not** a general accuracy claim.

## 4. Step 4 (bonus) — the pure de novo loop, also run locally

RFdiffusion generated a brand-new 30-residue backbone *from noise* (no template) in
**~0.77 min** on CPU; ProteinMPNN designed sequences for it; ESMFold checked whether each
folds back to that **invented** backbone (the only possible reference — there is no
native ground truth here).

| design | pLDDT | CA-RMSD vs RFdiffusion backbone | TM-score | verdict |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| denovo_design1 | 90.5 | **0.27 Å** | 0.96 | self-consistent ✅ |
| denovo_design2 | 78.7 | 0.40 Å | 0.92 | borderline ⚠️ (high agreement, lower confidence) |

## 6. Teaching points (straight from the numbers)

1. **Generation ≠ prediction.** ProteinMPNN wrote sequences only **19–62%** identical to
   the natural protein, yet most still fold back to the original shape — *many sequences,
   one fold*. ESMFold/AF2 did the inverse, *one sequence → its structure*. Two model
   types, one experiment.
2. **Validation here = self-consistency, not truth.** A low RMSD means "the predictor
   agrees the design should adopt the target fold." It does **not** prove folding in a
   tube. The signal is informative only because generator and predictor are
   independently trained (orthogonality) — but they can still share blind spots.
3. **Why you sample several designs and filter.** `1PGA_design1` failed (2.91 Å) while
   `1PGA_design2` passed (0.35 Å) from the *same* backbone — generation is stochastic, so
   you always produce several and keep the ones that pass.
4. **Harder cases are visible.** Villin HP36 stays borderline (pLDDT ~73–76, RMSD
   2.3–3.0 Å) and 1VII is the one native that AF/ESM reproduce least well — a built-in
   "where the loop struggles" lesson, not a failure to hide.
5. **No single metric.** Trp-cage redesigns score RMSD ~0.55 Å but TM-score only ~0.6;
   TM-score's `d0` is meaningless for ≤30-aa chains. Always read pLDDT + RMSD together and
   know each metric's domain.

## 7. Limitations & scope

- **Computational only.** No wet-lab folding/expression; "verified" = in-silico
  self-consistency (see scope banner and Methods §Scope).
- **Small, narrow sample.** 4 proteins, 20–76 aa, single-domain. Nothing here generalizes
  to large/multidomain proteins or complexes.
- **Predictor caveats.** Single-sequence ESMFold can be over/under-confident on hard
  cases (villin); MSA-based AF2 depends on alignment depth.
- **Metric caveats.** TM-score unreliable < ~30 aa; RMSD/TM computed under a fixed Kabsch
  superposition with ordinal CA mapping (not TM-align), see Methods §5.
- **CPU runtimes are not optimized** and are far faster on a CUDA GPU.
